Issuer Identification Number (IIN) Table

The first digit of any credit card number is referred to as the Major Industry Identifier (MII). Additionally, the initial six or eight digits of a credit card number, including the first MII digit, are collectively known as the Issuer Identification Number (IIN).

Bank Identification Numbers (BINs) are more commonly referred to as Issuer Identification Numbers (IINs). This nomenclature stems from the fact that cards can be issued by institutions beyond traditional banks.

The Issuer Identification Number (IIN) serves the crucial purpose of identifying the card issuing institution responsible for issuing the card to the cardholder. The card issuer reserves the remaining digits of the card number, with the card's length determined by the total number of digits. Many card issuers opt to include both the complete IIN and the account number on their cards.
